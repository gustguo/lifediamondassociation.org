Consumer Guide

What you need to know before commissioning a memorial diamond. Written by the independent certification authority, not a seller.

Before You Buy

Understand the Process

Memorial diamonds are real diamonds created from the carbon extracted from human hair or cremation ashes. They are chemically identical to natural diamonds — both are crystalline carbon. The difference is origin: one comes from the earth, the other from a person you loved.

Because this is an emotionally significant purchase, the industry is unfortunately vulnerable to misrepresentation. ILDA exists to help you distinguish legitimate laboratories from those that cut corners or make false claims.

Red Flags: When to Walk Away

ILDA's Consumer Protection Report identified the following warning signs in complaints:

  • No independent certification. The laboratory issues its own certificate without third-party verification. This is like grading your own exam.
  • Pressure to decide quickly. Memorial diamonds are not perishable. Any laboratory pushing for an immediate decision is prioritizing sales over your grief.
  • Unrealistic timelines. HPHT diamond synthesis takes 2-6 months. Claims of "2 weeks" suggest the diamond was pre-made and not grown from your specific material.
  • No chain-of-custody documentation. You should receive photographic evidence of your material at every stage of the process.
  • Refusal to provide spectroscopic data. Raman and FTIR spectra are the scientific proof that your diamond is real. A legitimate laboratory will share this data.
  • Price significantly below market. Memorial diamonds require specialized equipment and time. Prices below $2,000 per carat should raise questions about the source material.

What to Ask For

Before commissioning a memorial diamond, request the following from any laboratory:

  • ILDA accreditation number (verify it in our Member Directory)
  • Full spectroscopic report (Raman, FTIR, UV-Vis)
  • Chain-of-custody documentation with timestamps
  • Written guarantee of carbon source verification
  • Independent gemological grading report
  • Cooling-off period (minimum 48 hours) before final processing begins

Price Reference Guide (2025)

Memorial diamond pricing varies by region, laboratory, and service level. The following ranges are based on ILDA's analysis of published prices from accredited and pending laboratories:

0.25 ct $1,200 – $1,800 Entry option; limited color choices

1.00 ct $5,000 – $8,000 Standard milestone size

Price factors: Color grade (blue/yellow/clear), cut complexity, certification level (laboratory-issued vs. independent GIA/IGI), and whether the laboratory provides full chain-of-custody documentation. Prices below $2,000 for any carat weight should be treated with extreme caution.

Pre-Purchase Checklist

Print or save this checklist before contacting any laboratory:

□ Ask for the ILDA accreditation number and verify it at lifediamondassociation.org/directory.html

□ Request sample spectroscopic reports from previous clients (with identifying information redacted)

□ Confirm the minimum cooling-off period before processing begins (ILDA recommends 48 hours minimum)

□ Ask how they handle the case where insufficient carbon is extracted (reputable labs will refund or offer alternative)

□ Request a written timeline with milestones: material receipt → carbon extraction → synthesis start → completion → grading

□ Confirm whether the quoted price includes independent gemological grading (GIA/IGI) or only laboratory-issued certificates

□ Ask about insurance and shipping: is the diamond insured during transit? Who pays return shipping?

□ Request references from at least two previous clients (with permission)

Understanding the Science

Carbon Extraction: Hair contains approximately 45% carbon by dry weight. Cremation ashes contain 0.5-2.5% carbon. The extraction process must isolate this carbon with sufficient purity for diamond synthesis.

HPHT Synthesis: The extracted carbon is placed in a high-pressure press (typically 5-6 GPa, 1,300-1,600°C) where it crystallizes around a diamond seed. This process takes 2-6 months depending on desired carat weight.

Spectroscopic Verification: After synthesis, Raman spectroscopy confirms the sp³ carbon lattice structure. FTIR identifies nitrogen and boron content that distinguishes memorial diamonds from natural or industrial synthetic diamonds.

A Note on Emotional Vulnerability

ILDA recognizes that consumers seeking memorial diamonds are often grieving. This emotional state makes you vulnerable to high-pressure sales tactics and false promises. We encourage you to take your time, verify claims independently, and remember: a legitimate laboratory will never rush you. The person you are honoring deserves patience, not pressure.
