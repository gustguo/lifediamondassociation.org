Traceability Standards

The four-pillar protocol that ensures every ILDA-certified memorial diamond can be traced from source to certificate.

The Protocol

Quadruple-Verified Integrity

Every ILDA-certified diamond has passed through four independent verification stages. No single laboratory controls the entire process. No result is accepted without cross-validation.

Pillar I: Origin Validation

Before any carbon extraction begins, the source material must be registered in the ILDA Origin Chain System. This requires:

  • Photographic documentation of the source material at the point of handover
  • Cryptographic hashing of collection container seals with timestamps
  • Chain-of-custody logs signed at every transfer point
  • Independent witness verification for all high-value commissions

Origin validation is performed by an ILDA-certified third party, not the manufacturing laboratory itself.

Pillar II: Elemental Purity

The extracted carbon must meet strict purity thresholds before it can proceed to synthesis:

  • Carbon content ≥ 99.5% by mass spectrometry
  • Heavy metal contamination below 50 ppm
  • Isotopic profile consistent with documented biological origin
  • Independent laboratory verification at two separate facilities

Pillar III: HPHT Synthesis Audit

During the High-Pressure High-Temperature synthesis phase, ILDA monitors:

  • Press temperature and pressure logs, with deviations > 2% triggering automatic review
  • Seed crystal provenance documentation
  • Run duration and growth rate consistency checks
  • Quarterly unannounced on-site inspections by ILDA inspectors

Pillar IV: Spectroscopic Grading

The final certification requires non-destructive analysis by an ILDA-accredited gemological laboratory:

  • Raman spectroscopy to confirm sp³ carbon lattice structure
  • FTIR spectroscopy to detect nitrogen and boron content
  • UV-Vis spectroscopy for color origin verification
  • Photoluminescence mapping to identify synthetic vs. natural origin markers

All spectroscopic data is uploaded to the Global Registry, where certificate holders can access it at any time.

Protocol Revision Cycle

The ILDA Core Standard is reviewed annually by the Technical Advisory Committee and revised every three years. All revisions are published for public comment for 60 days before adoption. Member laboratories have 12 months to comply with new standards.
